the History of Steam

ISSUE 0001

The first commercial steam engine created in 1698 by the English inventor Thomas Savery.
Savery's engine could only see use as small engines, as large ones had the potential to
explode. His steam engine saw use in mines an supplying water wheels to power textile
machines.

Around 1712, the first engine successful in producing continuous power was invented by
English inventor Thomas Newcomen. This marked the beginning of piston steam engines.
From 1763 to 1775, Scottish inventor James Watt improved Newcomen's engine. His
early engine used air pressure from pistons instead of steam pressure. Watt's engine
used half the amount of coal as Newcomen's.

Steam engines were continuously developed throughout the 18th century. Several early
models of steam locomotives were invented in the 1780s. British inventor Richard
Trevithick created the first working railway steam locomotive, it's first journey transpiring
on February 21st, 1804.
